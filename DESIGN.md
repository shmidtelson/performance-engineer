# Design direction 2026 — Performance Engineer niche

**Chosen approach: Editorial Authority + Evidence**

For a solo Performance Engineer selling diagnostic calls to SaaS/e-commerce founders, the current (2026) fit is **not** agency wow, not AI-SaaS chrome, and not a cyan “dev dashboard” portfolio.

## Why this niche is different

You sell **judgment under production pressure**. Buyers (founders / CTOs / eng leads) scan for:

1. Do you understand my pain in one screen?
2. Can you prove outcomes with numbers?
3. Is the next step low-risk (short diagnostic)?
4. Does your own site feel fast, calm, and deliberate? (meta-proof)

Design that looks like a creative studio or a toy terminal fights that read.

## Approaches compared

| Approach | Look | Verdict for PE 2026 |
|----------|------|---------------------|
| **Creative agency** | Bento, GSAP, marquee, dark immersive | No — signals “design shop”, not reliability |
| **Dev dashboard portfolio** | Cyan cards, terminal UI, glass, glow | No — overplayed; reads junior / template |
| **Corporate consulting** | Stock handshake, blue gradients, 10 services | No — commodity |
| **Editorial Authority + Evidence** | Strong type, whitespace, person, metric-led cases, one CTA | **Yes** |

## What “Editorial Authority + Evidence” means

**Editorial (authority):**
- Large, confident headline (problem language)
- Face + name as product (solo brand)
- Generous spacing, F-pattern scan
- Numbered process / case rows — not identical card grids
- Minimal nav, one primary action

**Evidence (technical trust):**
- Metric strip early (LCP, throughput, uptime)
- Case blocks: before → after in mono as list rows (not metric cards)
- One interactive proof widget — editorial panel, not fake OS chrome
- **No** page-wide grids, glow orbs, or mist gradients (reads as AI-template)
- Site performance is part of the brand (Astro, little JS, CWV)

**Typography:** Instrument Serif (display) + Instrument Sans (UI) + JetBrains Mono (numbers) — quiet luxury / consulting authority, not fashion Didot

**CTA language:**
- Primary: solid ink / inverse white on dark band (product UI, not teal blob)
- Secondary: quiet ring / ghost
- Accent teal reserved for signals, links, focus — not every button fill

## What 2026 B2B guidance reinforces

- Outcome messaging over feature menus
- Proof before hard ask
- Purposeful simplicity over visual complexity
- Personal brand converts faster than fake “agency”
- Structure clear enough for humans *and* AI search citation (GEO)

## Explicitly out of scope for this brand

- Purple / glow / glassmorphism luxury
- Warm cream + terracotta editorial cliché
- Full-bleed dark-by-default “cool engineer”
- Pill-cluster CTAs, emoji, floating badges on hero
- Skill bars, Download CV, job-seeker chrome
- Heavy preloader / cursor effects

## Implementation note (current site)

We already lean this direction (`Production Clarity`). Next UI passes should **deepen editorial evidence** (type hierarchy, case as list rows, quieter chrome) rather than chase a new trend aesthetic.

**Confirm this direction** before a larger visual redesign.
